This Site Uses Cookies: What They Are and How They Can Be Used Against You

Published on September 2, 2024

In today’s digital landscape, the phrase “This site uses cookies” is universal. But what does it really mean? Cookies are small data files stored on your device by websites you visit. They are essential for enhancing your browsing experience but can also raise privacy and security concerns. This article explores what cookies are, their benefits and risks, and how to ensure your website complies with regulations like GDPR and CCPA.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by a website. They contain data about your browsing behavior, preferences, and login information.

The main types of cookies include:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser. They help websites remember your actions within a single session.
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period, even after closing your browser. They remember login details, language preferences, or items in your shopping cart.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by domains other than the one you are visiting, often used for tracking and advertising purposes.

How Cookies Can Be Used Against You

Despite their benefits, cookies can pose risks:

  • Privacy Invasion: Third-party cookies can track your activity across sites, creating a detailed profile without your consent. Example: Reports indicate that some cookies are programmed to follow you around the web, collecting information from your online behavior that’s used to build a detailed profile. This profile can reveal details such as whether you're a child, adult, engaged, or have children, where you live, your hobbies, job role, financial status, daily routines, entertainment choices, favorite brands, recent online searches, health concerns and political or social interests.
  • Targeted Advertising: Advertisers can use the data to display personalized ads based on your browsing history, potentially leading to a feeling of being constantly watched.

  • Security Risks: Cookies can sometimes be exploited to gain unauthorized access to your accounts. Example: Attackers can steal cookies from your device, move them to their laptop, and use them to log into your account from their own device, gaining access to your personal information.

Legal Obligations for Website Owners

Website owners must inform users about cookies and obtain their consent before placing cookies on their devices. This is crucial under laws like GDPR in the EU and CCPA in the USA. Here’s how to ensure compliance:

  • Implement a Cookie Banner: Display a banner informing users of cookie use and seeking their consent.
  • Update Your Privacy Policy: Clearly state the types of cookies used, what data they collect, and how it’s used. Provide options to manage cookie preferences.
  • Enable Cookie Management: Allow users to accept, reject, or customize cookie preferences and withdraw consent at any time.
